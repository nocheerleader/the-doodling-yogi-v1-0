About Me

I'm Dublin based Yoga teacher and illustrator; combining the two things I love most. When I began practicing yoga, I found myself intimidated in busy, packed studios. Through my teaching, I am always working to create small, intimate classes that feel safe for my students. This approach helps my students fully immerse themselves in their practice.

I also love to share my passion for Yoga through illustration. It began as a learning tool I used when studying new poses and sequences. I hope it can do the same for you too!

My Studies

I first became interested in Yoga and meditation in January 2015, when I completed an 8-week MBSR course with Helen Byrne, at The Mindfulness Centre. I fell in love and began practicing daily while reading and watching anything I could get my hands on.

I completed my 200-hour Yoga Teacher Training with Tara O'Neill at Vitality Traditional School of Yoga. I specialise in Restorative, Yin, Hatha, and meditation. I'm constantly studying and learning, finding exciting new techniques to share with my students.

Illustration

I've loved drawing ever since I was a child. So when I was able to combine this with Yoga it was a dream come true. I take custom requests from Yoga enthusiasts for their home practice space and from Yoga teachers for their studios.

I have been commissioned to create:

  • Posters for events and workshops
  • Illustrated infographs for poses
  • Gifts for Yoga lovers
  • Editorial illustrations

For further information on commissions or to receive a quote for a piece you would like made, contact me at hello@thedoodlingyogi.com

Meditation

We are all made of the elements of the earth. Our organs, bones, tissues, and our entire cell structure is made out of the vitamins and minerals that the vegetables, fruits, grains, nuts, seeds, and herbs of the earth provide for us when we eat them.

We are physically made from the earth. The plants biologically transmute the elements from the living soil of the earth up through their roots and naturally disperse them throughout their cell structure.

Our bodies are made of the elements of the Earth. And when we ground ourselves, we allow these energies to flow through us. Clearing, healing and balancing our minds, bodies, and hearts so that we can function properly and live happier, healthier and more balanced lives.

By grounding ourselves, we become fully present in our bodies, accepting the fact that even though we are spiritual beings, we are now walking on this Earth in this physical body, and we need to honor who we now are, the role we are meant to play and the path we chose to walk in this physical world.
